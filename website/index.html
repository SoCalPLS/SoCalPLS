SoCal Spring 2014: Programming Languages and Systems

The workshop will be held in Shanahan 3460, aka the SkyCube.
See this parking and building map.

The SoCal Programming Languages and Systems Workshop is an informal gathering of programming languages and systems researchers in Southern California. We meet next at Harvey Mudd College in Claremont, CA on Saturday May 10, 2014.

We have received generous sponsorships from Harvey Mudd College and its Computer Science Department.

Workshop on May 10

Goals

The primary goals of this workshop include
  • Providing a friendly environment for students (and other researchers) to present their work
  • Strengthening ties between local institutions
  • Learning about cool stuff happening in PL and systems!

Important Dates

April 25 Abstract deadline (end of day)
April 28 Notification of acceptance
May 1 Registration deadline
May 10 SoCal workshop

Submission Guidelines

Both original research and work that has been published in other venues is welcome.

Authors should submit abstracts of at most ~300 words. The submission deadline is Friday, April 25, 2014, by the end of the day.

Selected abstracts will be presented as a talk. The program committee will determine the length of the talks based on the number and content of the abstracts we receive; and they'll provide more information when they notify you of acceptance.
